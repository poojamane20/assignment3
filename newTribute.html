Mother Teresa

Born: 26 August 1910
Died: 5 September 1997
Nationality: Albanian, Indian, Ottoman, Yugoslav, Yugoslavian

Mother Teresa was the founder of the Order of the Missionaries of Charity, a Roman Catholic congregation of women dedicated to helping the poor. Considered one of the 20th Century's greatest humanitarians, she was canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta in 2016.

Mother Teresa was born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, the current capital of the Republic of Macedonia. In 1979, Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize for her humanitarian work. She died in September 1997 and was beatified in October 2003.
